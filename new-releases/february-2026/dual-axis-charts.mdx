title: "Dual-Axis Charts"
---

Dual-Axis Charts, also known as combo charts, are visualizations that combine multiple chart types or Y-axes into a single view[cite: 1788, 1789]. They allow you to display measures with different scales or units, such as absolute revenue alongside a percentage margin, by rendering each measure as a different series type[cite: 1790].

This feature ensures that metrics with different semantic meanings remain readable and helps you identify relationships between different data points that a single axis might obscure[cite: 1807, 1810, 1811]. This guide will walk you through the steps to understand how these charts are structured and how to use them to spot correlations in your data.

## How it Works

The system uses specific detection methods and structural components to build these rich visualizations.

### Detection Methods
The system identifies the need for a dual-axis chart in two ways:
* **Rule-based:** It recognizes keywords such as "combo chart," "dual axis," or "dual-axis" in your query[cite: 1806].
* **LLM-based:** The AI automatically detects when requested measures have different scales or semantic meanings[cite: 1807].

### Chart Structure
Each dual-axis chart is built using a specific layout specification:
* **Dual Y-axes:** Independent scales are provided for both the left and right Y-axes[cite: 1793].
* **Multiple Series Types:** Each measure can be rendered as a **COLUMN**, **BAR**, **LINE**, or **AREA**[cite: 1794, 1795, 1796, 1797, 1798].
* **Layout Mapping:** The system explicitly defines what data appears on the X-axis, which measures belong to each Y-axis, and the rendering position for each series[cite: 1799, 1800, 1801, 1802, 1803].

## Key Capabilities

Dual-Axis Charts allow you to perform advanced analysis within a single visualization:

* **Compare Different Scales:** View metrics with entirely different units, like transaction volume and conversion rates, in one place[cite: 1817, 1823].
* **Mix Visual Styles:** Combine bars and lines to distinguish between volume-based data and efficiency-based trends[cite: 1818, 1819].
* **Consolidate Insights:** Replace multiple standalone charts with a single visual that tells a more complete story[cite: 1813, 1820].
